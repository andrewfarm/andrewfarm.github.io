galaxies

A real-time simulation of tens of thousands of stars, forming galaxies that swirl and collide majestically in 3D.

boids

A real-time simulation of fish-like creatures schooling. An immersive, yet minimalistic world of predator and prey, interactive plants, and realistic behavior based on the original Boids algorithm by Craig Reynolds.

visualizer

A simple but elegant visualizer for audio, which also includes a real-time spectrogram. I'm currently trying to use the spectrogram data to transcribe music, so we'll see how that goes?

electrocity

A real-time simulation (god, another one?) of electrically charged objects interacting. This was atually the first simulation I wrote, and comes with visual features such as field lines (shown here), field-tracing particles and color map.

SCROBBLE

A game which is definitely completely my own invention, SCROBBLE is a crossword game - but instead of playing against you, it plays for you, intelligently finding the best possible play on any given turn.

edge

An edge detector. Nothing fancy, I suppose, but one of my first experiments with image processing.

Hover over the image on the left to see the effect!

MULTIPLAYER Triremes

The game is simple: ram the other player's boat. What's special about it? It is playable over a LAN, and was my first experiment with networked games. It is functional with any number of players and is actually quite addicting.

This was a collaboration with my brother, Ian.

Reverse Hangman

In this take on hangman, you come up with a word and the computer guesses! It's quite good at winning. (If you want to win, though, pick, like, a three-letter word with an O in the middle - there are a million of those, and it'll never guess the right one. That's how I lost a bet last year.)

Circle Invasion!

A classic arcade-style game. A square, who must fight against the advancing circles. Why does he fight? He does not know. There is no other way. There is only the crippling ennui that shrouds his limited existence. With each new wave the circles come with an increased will to live, which will be torn from them only seconds later. So has it been, and so will it continue, as ammunition depletes and the square and circle draw nearer both to the void. The square screams.
